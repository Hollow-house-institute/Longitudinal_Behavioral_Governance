# Relationship Between Longitudinal Behavioral Governance (LBG) and HHI-GOV-01

## Purpose

This document defines the scope boundary and authority relationship between **Longitudinal Behavioral Governance (LBG)** and **HHI-GOV-01**.

It exists to prevent overlap, confusion, or misinterpretation of roles between field definition and enforceable standard.

---

## Longitudinal Behavioral Governance (LBG)

**Role:** Field definition and governance rationale

LBG defines the **behavioral risk surface** that emerges over time when governance is present—or absent—in intelligent systems.

LBG explains:
- how behavior accumulates longitudinally,
- why defensive adaptation emerges under governance absence,
- and why policy, ethics, and point-in-time audits fail to control drift.

LBG is **descriptive and explanatory**, not prescriptive.

---

## HHI-GOV-01

**Role:** Enforceable governance standard

HHI-GOV-01 specifies the **minimum structural requirements** required to prevent the behavioral risks defined by LBG.

HHI-GOV-01 mandates:
- declared authority,
- decision boundaries,
- escalation ownership,
- safe refusal mechanisms,
- non-punitive exit protocols.

HHI-GOV-01 is **prescriptive and implementable**.

---

## Non-Replacement Statement

LBG does not replace HHI-GOV-01.  
HHI-GOV-01 does not supersede LBG.

They serve different but complementary functions.

---

## Canonical Relationship

> **LBG defines the behavioral risk surface.**  
> **HHI-GOV-01 specifies the minimum structural requirements to control it.**

This relationship is intentional and non-negotiable.
